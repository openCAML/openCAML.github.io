About Me


My name is Tom Hadfield and I'm a penultimate-year doctoral student in statistics at the University of Oxford in the UK. I'm a member of the Oxford Protein Informatics Group, supervised by Professor Charlotte Deane. My research focuses on the application of deep generative models to fragment-based drug design and is generously funded by the Engineering and Physical Sciences Research Council and my industrial collaborators Roche, UCB Pharma and LifeArc.

Prior to starting my doctorate I completed an MSc in Statistical Science (also at Oxford) and a BSc in Mathematics at the University of Bath.

Alongside my studies I am a tutor for the 3rd year undergraduate course in Statistical Machine Learning, run by the Department of Statistics. I play Badminton in the universities leagues for Oxford University Badminton Club and like to play Golf when the weather is nice.

How to get in touch

You can contact me via email at hadfield@stats.ox.ac.uk or via LinkedIn. If you spot a bug in my code or there's an algorithm not currently covered on this site that you'd like to see included, I'd be very happy to hear from you!
